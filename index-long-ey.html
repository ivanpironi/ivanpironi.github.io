Qiddiya — a sovereign KSA entertainment and tourism giga-programme with a capital development schedule extending to 2050 — had no reliable single source of truth across its delivery enterprise. Cost data in SAP and cost control systems produced irreconcilable figures. Schedule status in P6 could not be trusted at programme level. Document and engineering data lived in disconnected repositories. The development business had no confident view of where the programme stood — and no mechanism to build one.

This was not a technology implementation role. It was a senior advisory mandate — from digital needs assessment through platform selection through RFP governance through Nvidia Omniverse deployment. In a single engagement trajectory. On a live programme.

Stage 01 Digital Needs Assessment Advisory lead

Mapped the engineering and programme data landscape across the enterprise. Identified the root causes of data distrust: misaligned cost hierarchies between SAP and the cost control layer, no governed schedule baseline, and fragmented document control with no audit trail. Defined the information architecture requirements for a functioning PMIS.

→Defined the information architecture requirements for a functioning PMIS. Before anyone could fix the system, someone had to diagnose why the data could not be trusted. That is strategic work, not technical work.

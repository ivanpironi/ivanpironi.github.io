Strategic Partnerships

Two firms already inside
the programmes on this list.

Mace Consult and Worley are active delivery and engineering partners on several of the accounts in this plan. They hold the PMC or engineering mandate — EY holds the independent advisory layer above them.

Neither firm can objectively advise the owner on their own delivery performance. That is EY's structural position — and both firms have a commercial incentive to refer owner-side advisory work rather than attempt it themselves.

These are not prospective partnerships. The contacts listed are personal relationships — at Managing Director and Director level — that convert to warm introductions into programmes EY cannot currently enter through its own client base alone.

How the partnerships activate
Referral
Warm introduction to the owner

Mace or Worley introduces EY to the programme owner at the right seniority level. EY wins the advisory mandate independently. No cold approach. No pitch from zero — the relationship starts from a trusted referral.

Teaming
Joint response to a client mandate

EY leads the advisory workstream. Mace or Worley leads delivery or engineering. Single commercial proposal — each party bills its own scope. No conflict, no overlap.

Strategic Alliance
Named accounts · mutual commitment

Formalised across named GCC programmes as the relationship matures. Co-marketing, shared pipeline, joint client events. Revenue stays with the delivering party — the alliance governs access, not billing.
